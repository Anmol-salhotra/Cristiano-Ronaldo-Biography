The Great Scientist Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam


A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was the greatest scientist. His full name is Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam.

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (15 October 1931 – 27 July 2015) was an Indian aerospace scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born and raised in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu and studied physics and aerospace engineering. He worked with DRDO and ISRO, contributing to missile development.

[1]

Known as the Missile Man of India for his work on ballistic missiles and launch vehicles. Played a key role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998.

[2]

Elected as the 11th President of India in 2002. Called the "People’s President". Won awards including Bharat Ratna.

[3]

Passed away on 27 July 2015 while giving a lecture. Buried in Rameswaram with state honors.
